Space Weather Through the Eyes of Earthlings Although the Sun is 93 million miles away from our planet, solar activity can significantly impact our daily lives. “Space weather”.

Space weather is what happens when the Sun sends out energy and tiny particles into space. The Sun can have storms, called solar flares or solar eruptions, that shoot out light, heat, and charged particles. When this energy travels through space and reaches Earth, it can cause changes high up in our atmosphere and around our planet’s magnetic field. This is why we call it “weather in space,” even though there are no clouds or rain out there. Space weather can be both beautiful and dangerous. It creates the colorful auroras (Northern and Southern Lights) that glow in the sky, but it can also disturb satellites, mess up GPS or radios, and even affect power grids on Earth. Astronauts in space need protection too, because solar storms can be harmful to them. That’s why scientists study and predict space weather—so we can enjoy the beauty but also stay safe and keep our technology working.
